Singapore Polytechnic is situated in a exhilarating 94 acres of land, which has a wide range of flora and fauna. Since SP is an environmentally-consicious school, it seems learning and teaching buildings are located within a massive garden, which supports its students to explore and cherish nature. Having a big garden is not the only thing SP does in regards to environmental health. It has also partnered up with different non-profit organisations in effort to further develop susatinable energy, to encourage people to reduce waste, and to promote a green economy.

SP has a mind-blowing 230 species of flora and a variety of fauna.

SP has introduced CCC modules that focus on 17 SDGs.

SP students have enthusiastically participated in the 'SG Clean Day' event.

SP is now a 'smart campus' where sensors are constantly checking air quality.

SP's 'Environment Club' participate in and encourage environmental activities.

SP has launched the country's first-ever solar photovoltaics recycling line.
